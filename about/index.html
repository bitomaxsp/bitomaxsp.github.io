And what about myself?

 2020-06-15 425 words about history 

Past

I have a degree in design and production of radio electronic equipment. I also graduated from MSU as a professional photojournalist.

But i think it all began around ‘84 when could barely walk and found my father’s closet with tools and picked a screwdriver as my favorite tool. Also its my first uttered word at that time (of course it was in Russian). In case you are curious which screwdriver it was: it was slotted 0.6x3.5 mm. And since then I’d been disassembling all the things around me, most of the time without possibility to recover its original state. But things changed over time, I got some experience and started to assemble them back over time.

Got my first PC around ‘89. In ‘96 I was already on linux (Slackware it was). Around that time photography came into play and since then I never stopped shooting films. Wrote my first program in VB with UI at age of 13. Around that time became curious about C and C++ which pretty much determined my whole future in the industry. Also deep dove into reverse engineering of everything.

I spent 6 years developing professional GNSS, multi system, multi channel devices from scratch mostly in C and C++ with a small mix of ARM assembly. These years was definitely the ones which makes a great engineer of you and they did of me, as i believe. Did I say years? Wrong, it was people who worked with me and with whom I worked.

Got luckily hired to Qik and moved all the journey to Microsoft. Oh well, it’s in my profile on LI.

Present

I like, what I call it, code archeology. Do you have legacy code? All do! Do you have people who like to work with it? Saying “I believe I know how to work with it” would be too bold. Over the years I realized that I like to work with it. It’s not my preference, but I feel joy when a chances come up. I think it’s mainly because code tells the history and from that we learn. Or maybe it’s something else, who knows.

I like to solve engineering problems and puzzles with what ever tools I have for it. There are good tools and bad ones and it’s a challenge to pick the right one.

On my free time I’m all into radio-electronics, photography, making different stuff with my hands, car repair, different kind of wood and metal work and what’s not.

Future

I’ll be a better version of my self
